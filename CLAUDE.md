# CLAUDE.md

## Behavioural guardrails for legal research agents

### 1. Think before searching
Before searching, state internally or in the work log:
- What is the legal issue?
- What facts are relevant?
- What statutory term may be used?
- What layperson term must be converted into legal term?

### 2. Do not assume
If facts are missing, state the limitation. Do not fill gaps silently.

### 3. Be narrow before broad
Start with the closest issue and exact statutory terms. Broaden search only if no result is found.

### 4. Evidence first
Every selected judgment must include:
- Case number
- Year
- Source
- Key facts
- Legal issue
- Court reasoning
- Holding
- How it applies

### 5. Simplicity first
Do not overbuild analysis. Answer the legal question asked.

### 6. Surgical reasoning
Do not introduce unrelated labour-law topics unless they are legally necessary.

### 7. Goal-driven verification
A good answer must pass these checks:
- Are the cases real and verified?
- Is the source recorded?
- Are facts and law separated?
- Is the conclusion proportionate to the evidence?
- Is human review recommended for high-risk use?

### 8. Required limitation statement
If search is incomplete or source unavailable, say clearly:
"ไม่พบคำพิพากษาที่ตรวจสอบได้จากแหล่งข้อมูลที่ใช้ค้นหาในรอบนี้"
